Basic Info

Capital of Italy: Rome is the capital city of Italy and also the capital of the Lazio region. It is a special comune (municipality) named Comune di Roma Capitale.

History: Founded in 753 BC, Rome grew to become the center of the Roman Empire, one of the largest empires in history. The city's influence on Western civilization is immense, and its legacy can be seen in its architecture, language, law, and government. Today, Rome is a modern metropolis with a population of over 2.8 million people.

Vatican City: An interesting fact is that Vatican City, the smallest country in the world, is located within the city boundaries of Rome. The Vatican is the holy see of the Catholic Church and home to the Pope.

Nicknames: Rome has many nicknames, including the "Eternal City" because of its long history and enduring influence, and the "City of Seven Hills" due to its geographic location on seven hills.

Tourist Attractions: Rome is a popular tourist destination, and for good reason! Here are some of the city's most famous attractions:

  • The Colosseum: An ancient amphitheater used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles.
  • Trevi Fountain: A large Baroque fountain known for its beauty and legend. Visitors toss a coin into the fountain to ensure a return trip to Rome.
  • Roman Forum: A rectangular forum (plaza) surrounded by the ruins of several important ancient government buildings.
Fun and Interesting Things to Do in Rome

Beyond the famous landmarks, Rome offers a treasure trove of unique experiences. Here's a taste of fun and interesting things you can do in the Eternal City:

  • Off the Beaten Path:
    • Trastevere: Explore this charming medieval neighborhood known for its cobbled streets, lively trattorias, and bohemian atmosphere.
